Predictive Maintenance

    Description

    In this project, I conceived, developed, and implemented an advanced predictive maintenance program targeting various HVAC motors. The program harnesses live data, motor design specifications, and predictive modeling techniques to proactively detect equipment degradation and suboptimal performance. The primary objective is to mitigate costly and unforeseen equipment failures within HVAC systems.

    Methods and Tools

    In this portfolio, I showcase my proficiency in developing a state-of-the-art predictive maintenance program. Here's a brief breakdown of the key methods and tools used:

    1. Real-Time Monitoring: Implementing real-time monitoring allows for the continuous collection of crucial HVAC motor performance metrics. This live data stream serves as the foundation for early anomaly detection and proactive decision-making.
    2. Historical Data Analysis: Through in-depth historical data analysis, I uncover valuable insights, identifying trends and anomalies in past motor behavior. This historical context is pivotal for understanding equipment evolution.
    3. Data Preprocessing: My data preprocessing skills ensure the reliability and quality of the dataset. By cleaning, transforming, and enhancing the data, I guarantee its suitability for predictive modeling by eliminating stagnant, and out of range data.
    4. Predictive Modeling: I leverage advanced predictive modeling techniques to forecast equipment performance. My models, trained on historical data trends and design specifications, are pivotal in detecting anomalies and predicting potential failures.
    5. Feature Engineering: My proficiency in feature engineering enhances predictive accuracy by creating pertinent features from the dataset. This process enriches the data, allowing models to capture nuanced patterns effectively.

    This portfolio highlights my contribution to optimizing maintenance strategies, reducing downtime, and enhancing equipment longevity through the application of these critical methods and tools.

    Results

    After the model was built, 8 pieces of equipment were selected for vibration testing to verify the results of the program. The model predicted the failing equipment with an 87% accuracy rate (7 of 8). Seven of units were found to have severe bearing fatigue or motor failure and were scheduled for replacement or repairs.

    Here is a sample of the graphic that was used to identify the degrading equipment. We are able to filter by the equipment, Motor HZ, and timestamp in order to visualize the score over time, as well as the score for each bin Motor HZ. We generally noticed the score would decrease in the upper motor speeds first and slowly transition to all motor speeds.

    Code Sample

    While the actual code samples are unavailable, this outline offers a clear overview of the data flow and the key steps involved in the project's technical aspects. Your understanding of this limitation is greatly appreciated as we strive to provide valuable insights into our engineering work.

    1. Secure Connection: Initially, we established a secure connection between CICS (BMS Management System) and SQL, where all data processing took place.
    2. Data Integrity Check: As an essential step, we conducted a data integrity check to identify and flag any anomalies. This process included detecting out-of-range values, stagnant data, and verifying the equipment's operational status.
    3. Integration of Design Data: We integrated design data into the system to perform back calculations. This allowed us to estimate the KW (kilowatts) each motor was expected to use at a given motor speed or airflow, leveraging the principles of the first fan law.
    4. Score Calculation: Using both predicted and actual KW values, we calculated a score on a scale ranging from 0 to 10. This score served as an indicator of equipment health and performance.
    5. Power BI Dashboard: The calculated scores were then inserted into a Power BI Dashboard, providing a visual representation of equipment status and performance trends.

    Next Steps

    To further enhance this project further the following functionality would greatly increase the tool's value.

    1. Integrating a lifetime data pool that will allow us to begin estimating the lifetime of use based on the predictive maintenance score. Added integration with vibrations testing should serve to further the utility and accuracy of this tool as a Predictive and Preventative platform but will need to be validated over time through pilot projects.
    2. Generating an estimated repair cost associated with the faulty parts and equipment to assist with long-term budgeting. Here is an example of a demo dashboard that was put together in preparation to pitch the idea.
